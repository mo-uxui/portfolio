Protecting Nature Together: Why Protected Areas Matter for Our Future

Protected areas are vital today because they help protect wildlife, forests, and oceans from damage while also supporting healthy air, water, and soil. With growing threats like climate change and deforestation, these areas make sure nature and people can both have a safer future.

Protected areas also matter for people. They create jobs, support tourism, and protect cultural and Indigenous heritage. With good planning and community involvement, they bring long-term benefits for both nature and society.

  1. Conserving biodiversity and protecting endangered species
  2. Maintaining clean air, fresh water, and healthy soils
  3. Helping reduce climate change impacts by storing carbon
  4. Supporting local economies through tourism and sustainable jobs
  5. Protecting cultural and Indigenous heritage sites
  6. Providing spaces for recreation, education, and future research
